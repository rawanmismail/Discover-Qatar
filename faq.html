Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions visitors have about travelling to Qatar.

Do I need a visa to visit Qatar?
Nationals of over 100 countries can receive a free visa on arrival in Qatar, valid for 30 days and extendable once. Citizens of other countries can apply for an e-Visa online through the official Qatar e-Visa portal. Transit passengers are eligible for a free 96-hour transit visa.
Is Qatar safe for tourists?
Qatar is consistently ranked among the safest countries in the world. Crime rates are extremely low, the healthcare system is excellent, and emergency services are reliable and professional. Visitors of all backgrounds are welcomed warmly.
What is the best time to visit Qatar?
The best time to visit Qatar is from October to April, when temperatures are mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. The summer months (May to September) are very hot, with temperatures regularly exceeding 40 degrees Celsius, but indoor attractions and excellent hotel rates make this period worthwhile for some visitors.
Can I drink alcohol in Qatar?
Alcohol is available in Qatar but is restricted to licensed venues such as five-star hotels, certain restaurants, and licensed clubs. It is not sold in supermarkets or consumed in public places. Visitors are expected to drink responsibly.
What should I wear in Qatar?
Qatar is a Muslim country and modest dress is expected in public. Shoulders and knees should generally be covered when in public areas such as souqs, government buildings, and mosques. Swimwear is acceptable at hotel pools and beach resorts. Western dress standards are generally followed in shopping malls and hotels.
What currency does Qatar use?
Qatar uses the Qatari Riyal (QAR), which is pegged to the US Dollar at a rate of approximately 3.64 QAR per USD. Credit and debit cards are widely accepted throughout the country. ATMs are readily available in all major areas.
Is Qatar LGBTQ+ friendly?
Qatar is a conservative Muslim country where same-sex relationships are not legally recognised. All visitors are welcomed and respected, but public displays of affection are discouraged for all couples regardless of orientation. We recommend that all visitors familiarise themselves with local laws and customs before travelling.
How do I get around Qatar?
Qatar has an excellent public transport system including the Doha Metro, public buses, and widely available taxis. The Karwa taxi app and Uber also operate in Qatar. Car hire is available from all major international companies. Driving is on the right-hand side and roads are generally well maintained.
What language is spoken in Qatar?
Arabic is the official language of Qatar. English is widely spoken in hotels, restaurants, shops, tourist attractions, and business settings, making it straightforward for English-speaking visitors to navigate the country without Arabic language skills.
Are there family-friendly activities in Qatar?
Qatar is an excellent family destination with a wide range of child-friendly activities. These include theme parks, water parks, interactive museums, beach resorts, desert safaris, and cultural experiences. The country is safe, clean, and well-equipped with family-friendly facilities throughout.
