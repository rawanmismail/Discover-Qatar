About Mesaieed

Qatar's Gateway to the Inland Sea

Mesaieed, also known as Umm Said, is an industrial city located approximately 45 kilometres south of Doha. It is home to Qatar's largest petrochemical and industrial complex, including Qapco, Qatar Steel, and Qatar Fertiliser Company, facilities that have played a central role in the country's economic development.

Yet Mesaieed is far more than an industrial centre. The city is the primary gateway to two of Qatar's most breathtaking natural attractions: Sealine Beach and Khor Al Adaid, the famous Inland Sea. These destinations make Mesaieed one of the most visited destinations in Qatar outside of Doha, attracting visitors in search of sand, sea, and desert adventure.

Sealine Beach

Sealine Beach is Qatar's most popular resort beach, a wide, sandy stretch of Gulf coastline backed by rolling dunes. The Sealine Beach Resort offers full facilities including sun loungers, water sports, restaurants, and accommodation, but the beach itself is accessible to all visitors. The combination of golden sand, turquoise water, and dramatic dune backdrops creates one of Qatar's most photogenic landscapes.

Khor Al Adaid — The Inland Sea

Khor Al Adaid, or the Inland Sea, is one of Qatar's most extraordinary natural wonders and is recognised by UNESCO as an Important Natural Area. This remarkable tidal inlet penetrates deep into the Qatari desert from the south-east, creating a dramatic landscape where towering sand dunes rise directly from the water's edge. The site can only be reached by 4x4 vehicle, making a guided desert safari the ideal way to experience this unique environment.

Desert Adventures

The dunes surrounding Mesaieed and Khor Al Adaid are among the finest in the country for off-road adventures. Tour operators run regular dune bashing excursions, sandboarding sessions, and overnight camping experiences in the desert. Watching the sun set over the Inland Sea, surrounded by towering dunes, is one of the most unforgettable experiences Qatar has to offer.
