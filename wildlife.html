Natural Qatar

Flora and Fauna

Despite its arid climate, Qatar supports a surprisingly rich variety of wildlife across its desert, coastal, and marine environments.

Marine Life

The Arabian Gulf's Marine Ecosystem

Qatar's coastal waters are home to an extraordinary diversity of marine life. Dugongs, sometimes called sea cows, graze on the extensive seagrass meadows of the Gulf. Hawksbill and green sea turtles nest on Qatar's beaches. Dolphins are frequently sighted in coastal waters, and seasonal whale shark visits delight divers and snorkellers.

Desert Wildlife

Fauna of the Desert Interior

The Qatari desert, seemingly austere, is home to a remarkable range of adapted creatures. The Arabian oryx, Qatar's national animal, has been successfully reintroduced after near extinction. Sand gazelles, cape hares, and desert foxes roam the interior. Numerous species of lizard and snake have evolved to thrive in the harsh desert conditions.

Birdlife

Qatar — A Birdwatcher's Paradise

Qatar lies on the East Atlantic Flyway, making it an important stopover for millions of migratory birds. Al Thakira Mangroves near Al Khor provide critical habitat for flamingos, herons, and cormorants. The Ras Abrouq peninsula in the west attracts rare species of waders and terns. Over 300 bird species have been recorded in Qatar.

Conservation

Protecting Qatar's Natural Heritage

Arabian Oryx Sanctuary

A protected reserve dedicated to the conservation and breeding of the Arabian oryx, Qatar's national animal.

Al Thakira Mangroves

Protected mangrove forests providing nesting habitat for rare waterbirds and nursery grounds for fish.

Coral Conservation

Active programmes to monitor and protect Qatar's coral reef ecosystems from bleaching and human impact.

Sea Turtle Protection

Nesting sites for hawksbill and green sea turtles are protected and monitored by conservation teams.
