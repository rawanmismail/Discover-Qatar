Cultural Qatar

Museums, Galleries and Heritage Sites

MIA

Museum of Islamic Art

The Museum of Islamic Art is one of Qatar's most iconic landmarks and houses one of the finest collections of Islamic art in the world. Designed by the legendary architect I.M. Pei and perched on a promontory overlooking Doha Bay, the museum spans 1,400 years of Islamic civilisation across its spectacular galleries. The building itself is a masterpiece of contemporary architecture.

Learn More
NMQ

National Museum of Qatar

The National Museum of Qatar, designed by Jean Nouvel in the shape of a desert rose, tells the story of Qatar from its ancient geological origins to its extraordinary present. Interactive exhibitions, spectacular audio-visual displays, and a remarkable collection of artefacts take visitors on a compelling journey through the Qatari experience.

Learn More
Katara

Katara Cultural Village

Katara Cultural Village is Qatar's premier cultural hub, a beautifully designed complex housing galleries, a 5,000-seat amphitheatre, art workshops, restaurants, and a mosque modelled on the great mosques of Andalusia. Regular events including film festivals, art exhibitions, and musical performances make Katara a vibrant centre of creative life.
