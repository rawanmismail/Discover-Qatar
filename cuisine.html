Taste Qatar

The Flavours of Qatari Cuisine

Qatari food is a celebration of communal living, generous portions, and aromatic spices that have travelled across continents.

Traditional Food

Machboos — The National Dish

Machboos is Qatar's national dish: a fragrant rice cooked with meat or fish, enriched with a blend of spices including cinnamon, cardamom, dried limes (loomi), and rose water. Slow-cooked to perfection, it is typically served family-style on a large communal platter, embodying the spirit of Qatari hospitality.

Street Food

Harees and Balaleet

Harees, a comforting dish of slow-cooked wheat and meat, is a staple at family gatherings and celebrations. Balaleet, sweetened vermicelli noodles paired with a savoury omelette, is a beloved breakfast dish that perfectly captures the sweet-savoury complexity of Qatari cooking.

Learn More
Sweets

Arabic Sweets and Coffee

No meal in Qatar is complete without the ritual of Arabic coffee and dates. Luqaimat, golden deep-fried dumplings drizzled with date syrup, and muhallabia, a delicate rosewater milk pudding, are among the most cherished traditional desserts served throughout the country.
