About Al Khor

Qatar's Charming Northern City

Al Khor, located approximately 57 kilometres north of Doha, is one of Qatar's most beloved coastal cities. With a population of around 80,000 people, it retains a warmth and intimacy that contrasts beautifully with the bustle of the capital. The city is centred around a natural creek — the Khor — which gives it its name, and which today forms the heart of a beautiful corniche and public park.

Historically, Al Khor was a significant pearling and fishing town. Evidence of this heritage is visible throughout the city, from the restored traditional houses lining the old waterfront to the remnants of ancient watchtowers that once stood guard over the settlement. Today the city serves as an important administrative and residential centre for northern Qatar.

Al Khor Corniche and Park

The Al Khor Corniche is arguably the finest in Qatar outside of Doha. Stretching along the inlet, it offers a beautifully landscaped promenade with playgrounds, cafes, and stunning views of the calm, sheltered waters. The adjacent Al Khor Park is a popular family destination with boating lakes, a small zoo, mini train rides, and extensive green spaces.

Al Thakira Mangroves

Just a short distance from the city centre lies one of Qatar's most extraordinary natural treasures: the Al Thakira Mangroves. This protected area of ancient mangrove forest can be explored by kayak or on guided boat tours, with the opportunity to spot flamingos, herons, and other waterbirds nesting among the dense, twisted roots. It is an unmissable experience for nature lovers visiting northern Qatar.

Getting to Al Khor

Al Khor is easily reached from Doha by car via the Al Shamal Road (approximately 45 minutes). Public buses also connect the city to the capital. The drive north passes through open desert, offering a pleasant sense of escape from the urban environment.

What to Do in Al Khor

Kayaking the Mangroves

Glide silently through the ancient Al Thakira Mangrove forest by kayak, watching for flamingos and herons along the way.

Corniche Walks

Stroll or cycle along the beautiful corniche promenade, enjoying views across the sheltered inlet at sunrise or sunset.

Al Khor Park

A wonderful family outing with boating, a small zoo, miniature train rides, and extensive play areas for children.

Al Khor Museum

A small but fascinating museum housed in a traditional fort, documenting the pearling and fishing heritage of the city.
