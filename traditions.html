Living Heritage

Qatar's Rich Cultural Traditions

Rooted in Bedouin and pearling heritage, Qatari traditions continue to thrive and evolve in the modern era.

Falconry

The ancient art of falconry holds a revered place in Qatari culture. Recognized by UNESCO, it remains a symbol of heritage, patience, and the deep bond between humans and nature.

Camel Racing

Once a practical necessity, camel racing is now a beloved sport and cultural spectacle, with races held at dedicated tracks across the country.

Pearl Diving

The tradition of pearl diving shaped Qatari society for centuries. Today it is commemorated in museums, festivals, and the iconic image of the pearl on the Corniche.

Arabic Calligraphy

A revered art form throughout the Islamic world, Arabic calligraphy is celebrated in Qatar through galleries, workshops, and public installations.

Arabic Coffee and Dates

Serving qahwa (Arabic coffee) with dates is the quintessential expression of Qatari hospitality and is offered to every guest as a gesture of welcome.

Traditional Music

Qatari music traditions including Leiwah, Fijiri, and Al Ardha reflect the country's rich cultural tapestry of African, Persian, and Arab influences.

Dress and Identity

Traditional Qatari Dress

Traditional Dress

The Thobe and Abaya

Traditional dress remains an important part of Qatari identity. Men traditionally wear the thobe, a long white robe, paired with the ghutra (headscarf) and agal (black cord). Women traditionally wear the abaya, a full-length black cloak, often beautifully embroidered. Visitors are encouraged to dress modestly, particularly when visiting mosques, cultural sites, and traditional souqs.
