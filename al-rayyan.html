About Al Rayyan

Qatar's Second City — A Nation's Ambition in Built Form

Al Rayyan is the most populous municipality in Qatar after the capital, home to more than 700,000 residents spread across a vast arc of territory to the west and south-west of Doha. It encompasses a remarkable diversity of environments: established residential suburbs, cutting-edge educational institutions, iconic sporting venues, major retail destinations, open desert, and the rapidly expanding communities of its newer western districts.

For visitors to Qatar, Al Rayyan offers an entirely different perspective from the historic character of Doha's souqs or the coastal charm of towns like Al Wakra. This is Qatar looking forward, building the future — and doing so with extraordinary ambition, investment, and architectural courage. The 2022 FIFA World Cup gave Al Rayyan its greatest moment of global visibility, with three of the tournament's most celebrated venues located within its boundaries.

Education City — Qatar's Campus of the Future

Education City is arguably Qatar's single most impressive and transformative development project. This sprawling 14-square-kilometre campus, located in the heart of Al Rayyan municipality, was conceived as an international centre of academic excellence and research — a place where the world's greatest universities would bring their expertise to Qatar and help build the knowledge economy the country needs for its post-oil future.

Today, Education City hosts branch campuses of six of the world's most prestigious universities: Georgetown University (School of Foreign Service), Northwestern University (Journalism and Communication), Weill Cornell Medicine-Qatar, Carnegie Mellon University (Computer Science and Business), Texas A&M University (Engineering), and HEC Paris (Business). The student body of several thousand represents over 100 nationalities, making it one of the most international academic communities anywhere on earth.

Beyond the universities, the campus contains the Qatar National Library — a stunning building designed by Dutch architect Rem Koolhaas that has been described as one of the finest library buildings built anywhere in the world in the 21st century. The library's vast, undulating interior, its collection of over a million items, and its striking heritage collection of rare Arabic manuscripts make it a destination in itself.

The campus is also home to the Qatar Science and Technology Park, the Oxygen Park (a beautifully landscaped public green space powered entirely by solar energy), and numerous research institutes working on challenges ranging from renewable energy to advanced medicine. The architecture of Education City is remarkable in its variety and ambition — each building is a statement, collectively they form one of the most architecturally significant campuses in the world.

Visiting Education City

Education City is open to the public and visitors are welcome to walk the campus and visit the Qatar National Library. The library is open Saturday–Thursday 8:00 AM–9:00 PM and Friday 1:00 PM–9:00 PM. Entry to the library is free. The campus can be reached via the Doha Metro (Green Line, Education City Station).

World Cup Stadiums — Qatar's Sporting Cathedrals

The 2022 FIFA World Cup left Al Rayyan with three extraordinary sporting venues that rank among the finest in the world. All three are now in active use for domestic football, international matches, concerts, and events, and all welcome visitors for behind-the-scenes stadium tours on non-match days.

Al Rayyan SC Home Ground

Ahmad Bin Ali Stadium

Completely rebuilt from scratch for the 2022 World Cup, Ahmad Bin Ali Stadium is one of the most visually striking venues in Qatar. Its dramatic facade of interlocking geometric panels, inspired by the desert landscape, Qatari cultural patterns, and the architecture of the dunes, creates a building that seems to glow from within when illuminated after dark. With a capacity of 40,000 during the World Cup — now reconfigured to 21,000 — the stadium serves as the proud home of Al Rayyan Sports Club, one of Qatar's most successful football teams.

Sports in Qatar
The Diamond of the Desert

Education City Stadium

Known as the Diamond in the Desert for its spectacular faceted exterior, the Education City Stadium sits at the heart of the Education City campus and has quickly become one of the most celebrated stadiums of the 2022 World Cup. The stadium's triangular geometric exterior panels shift in colour and intensity depending on the angle of the sun, creating an ever-changing appearance that has made it one of the most photographed buildings in Qatar. Post-tournament, its upper tiers have been donated to developing countries in need of sporting infrastructure, reducing its capacity to a more intimate 20,000.

World Cup Final Venue

Lusail Stadium

The grandest of all Qatar's World Cup venues, Lusail Stadium hosted the tournament final between Argentina and France on 18 December 2022 — the most watched sporting event in human history. With a capacity of 88,966, it is the largest stadium in Qatar and the entire Arab world. Its golden exterior, inspired by the traditional fanned bowls and vessels of Islamic art and architecture, makes it one of the most instantly recognisable sporting venues on earth. Located on the boundary of Al Rayyan and the new city of Lusail, it is a short metro ride from central Doha.

Shopping, Dining & Entertainment

Al Rayyan is home to some of Qatar's finest shopping and entertainment destinations, offering visitors a very different experience from the traditional souqs of Doha or Al Wakra.

Mall of Qatar

Qatar's largest shopping and entertainment destination, with over 600 stores, a full-scale theme park (Al Rugaib), a 20-screen cinema, an ice rink, and dozens of dining options across a vast, beautifully designed complex.

Al Rayyan Park

One of Qatar's most popular public parks, offering extensive green lawns, cycling and jogging tracks, sports courts, play areas, and shaded seating for families and outdoor enthusiasts.

Education City Golf Club

A championship 18-hole golf course set within the Education City campus, open to visitors and home to regular professional and amateur tournaments throughout the year.

Aspire Zone

The Aspire Zone sports complex on the eastern edge of Al Rayyan offers world-class athletic facilities, an aquatic centre, and the Aspire Tower — a 300-metre landmark visible from across the city.

Planning Your Visit to Al Rayyan

Al Rayyan's main attractions are spread across a large area. A car or taxi gives the most flexibility, though the Doha Metro's Green Line connects several key destinations.

Doha Metro

The Green Line serves Education City Station and connects to Doha's central metro network. The Red Line serves Lusail Stadium from the airport and central Doha.

By Car

Most attractions are 15–25 minutes from central Doha by car. Parking is free and ample at all major venues and the Mall of Qatar.

Time Needed

Education City and the National Library alone could fill a half day. Add a stadium tour or a visit to the Mall and you have a full day in Al Rayyan.

Stadium Tours

Tours of Ahmad Bin Ali Stadium and Education City Stadium are available on non-match days. Check the Qatar Football Association website for schedules.

Architectural Masterpiece

The Qatar National Library

Designed by the legendary Dutch architect Rem Koolhaas of OMA, the Qatar National Library is one of the most extraordinary public buildings in the world. Its form — a vast diamond-shaped structure with an undulating floor that rises at the edges to become walls and ceiling — creates an interior that is simultaneously intimate and monumental.

The library holds over one million items including books, digital resources, and one of the most significant collections of rare Arabic manuscripts in existence. The Heritage Library at its centre houses manuscripts, maps, and documents of extraordinary historical significance, displayed in a dedicated conservation environment.

Admission to the Qatar National Library is completely free and it is open to all residents and visitors. It stands as one of the finest arguments for the transformative power of public investment in knowledge and culture.
