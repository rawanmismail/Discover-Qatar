Travel Safety

Qatar — One of the World's Safest Destinations

Crime

Exceptionally Low Crime Rates

Qatar has one of the lowest crime rates in the world. Violent crime is extremely rare, and petty theft, while not entirely absent, is uncommon. Visitors can generally feel safe walking in cities and public spaces, including at night. The presence of police is visible and the country's law enforcement is considered professional and helpful.

Learn More
Laws

Understanding Local Laws

Qatar is an Islamic country and visitors are expected to respect local customs and laws. Public consumption of alcohol is restricted to licensed venues such as hotels and certain restaurants. Modest dress is expected in public areas, particularly outside resort environments. Drugs are strictly prohibited and penalties are severe. Public displays of affection should be avoided.

Health and Emergency

Staying Safe and Healthy

Emergency Services

Dial 999 for police, ambulance, or fire services. Operators speak Arabic and English.

Healthcare

Qatar has excellent public and private healthcare facilities. Travel insurance with health coverage is strongly recommended.

Medication

Carry prescription medications with official documentation. Some substances legal elsewhere may be restricted in Qatar.

Heat Safety

During summer, take precautions against extreme heat. Stay hydrated, seek shade, and avoid prolonged outdoor exposure.

Water

Tap water is technically safe but bottled water is widely available and preferred by most visitors.

Consular Support

Register with your home country's consulate or embassy upon arrival for assistance in emergencies.
