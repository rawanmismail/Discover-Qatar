Top Travel Tips for Qatar

Dress Code

Dress modestly in public, covering shoulders and knees. Swimwear is acceptable at hotel pools and beaches only.

Currency

The Qatari Riyal (QAR) is the official currency. Credit cards are widely accepted. ATMs are plentiful throughout Doha.

Language

Arabic is the official language, but English is widely spoken in hotels, restaurants, shops, and tourist areas.

Climate

The best weather for outdoor activities is October to April. Summer temperatures can exceed 40°C; plan indoor activities accordingly.

Connectivity

Qatar has excellent mobile coverage and affordable SIM cards available at the airport and convenience stores.

Ramadan

During Ramadan, eating, drinking, and smoking in public during daylight hours is prohibited. Plan accordingly if visiting during this period.

Transport Tips

Use the Karwa app for taxis. Download the Qatar Rail app for metro routes. Ride-sharing apps are also widely available.

Photography

Ask permission before photographing local people. Avoid photographing government buildings and military installations.

Cultural Etiquette

Respecting Qatari Culture

Hospitality

The Art of Qatari Hospitality

Qatari culture places extraordinary value on hospitality. If invited to a Qatari home, it is customary to remove shoes at the door and accept food or drink when offered. Declining refreshments can be considered impolite. The right hand is traditionally used for eating and greeting. Greetings between men may involve a light nose-to-nose touch in the traditional Arab style.
