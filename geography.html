The Physical Geography of Qatar

Explore the diverse natural landscapes that shape this remarkable peninsula in the Arabian Gulf.

Terrain

The Qatari Peninsula

Qatar occupies a flat, arid peninsula stretching approximately 160 kilometres from north to south and 80 kilometres from east to west at its widest point. The terrain is predominantly low-lying desert, with elevations rarely exceeding 103 metres above sea level at Qurayn Abu al Bawl, the country's highest point.

Coastline

Miles of Pristine Coastline

Qatar is blessed with an extensive coastline stretching over 560 kilometres. The western coast along the Gulf of Bahrain is characterized by sandy beaches, shallow bays, and salt flats. The eastern coast, facing the Arabian Gulf, features rocky shores, coral reefs, and a richly diverse marine ecosystem.

Desert

The Inland Sea and Sand Dunes

Khor Al Adaid, often referred to as the Inland Sea, is one of Qatar's most spectacular natural wonders. Located in the south-east of the country, this UNESCO-recognized natural reserve features towering sand dunes meeting a tidal inlet of the Arabian Gulf, creating a landscape of breathtaking beauty.

Natural Features

Notable Geographical Landmarks

Khor Al Adaid

The famous Inland Sea, where the desert meets the sea in a spectacle of natural wonder.

Jebel Fuwairit

The northern highlands featuring limestone ridges and cliffs overlooking the Gulf.

Al Thakira Mangroves

Protected mangrove forests providing vital habitat for migratory birds and marine life.

Coral Reefs

Rich underwater ecosystems supporting over 700 species of fish and marine organisms.

Rub Al Khali Border

Qatar's southern reaches touch the edge of the famous Empty Quarter desert.

Underground Aquifers

Ancient freshwater reserves beneath the desert that have sustained life for millennia.
