Overview

Welcome to the State of Qatar

Qatar is a small yet extraordinarily dynamic nation located on a peninsula extending into the Arabian Gulf. With a population of over 2.9 million people and a land area of approximately 11,571 square kilometres, Qatar punches well above its weight on the world stage, thanks to vast natural gas reserves, visionary leadership, and an ambitious national development strategy.

The country is officially known as the State of Qatar and is governed as an absolute monarchy under the Al Thani royal family. The current Emir, Sheikh Tamim bin Hamad Al Thani, has continued the transformative vision that placed Qatar firmly on the global map.

A Nation on the Rise

Qatar has undergone one of the most remarkable transformations in modern history. Just decades ago, the country relied primarily on pearl diving and fishing. Today it is home to the world's third-largest natural gas reserves and boasts one of the highest per capita incomes globally.

The hosting of the 2022 FIFA World Cup served as a defining moment for Qatar, introducing millions of visitors to its warmth, culture, and world-class infrastructure. The legacy of that tournament continues to benefit residents and tourists alike.

Culture and Society

Qatari society is deeply rooted in Islamic values and Bedouin traditions, yet it is remarkably open and welcoming to people of all backgrounds. The country is home to over 100 nationalities, making it one of the most diverse nations in the world. Visitors will find a culture that values generosity, hospitality, and respect above all else.

  • Area: 11,571 km²
